# Airworthiness Directives — Applicable to Indian Operators

## Document Reference
- **Issuing Authority**: DGCA India (with reference to FAA, EASA, and OEM service bulletins)
- **Applicability**: All Indian-registered aircraft of the types specified
- **Compilation Date**: 15 March 2026
- **Status**: Active and enforceable

---

## AD-2026-B787-001: Boeing 787-8/787-9 Engine N2 Vibration Monitoring

**Effective Date**: 01 February 2026
**Applicability**: All Boeing 787-8 and 787-9 aircraft equipped with GEnx-1B or Trent 1000 engines.
**Supersedes**: None (new AD)

### Background
Reports from multiple operators have identified instances of elevated N2 (high-pressure rotor) vibration levels during cruise flight, particularly at high altitude and high thrust settings. Investigation by the engine manufacturers has identified potential bearing degradation that, if unchecked, could lead to uncontained engine failure.

### Required Actions
1. **Immediate Inspection Threshold**: If the Engine Vibration Monitoring System (EVMS) records an N2 vibration level exceeding **3.0 mm/s** (RMS) during any phase of flight, the aircraft shall be removed from service and the affected engine shall undergo a borescope inspection of the N2 turbine bearing assembly within **25 flight hours** of the exceedance event.
2. **Recurring Monitoring**: For all aircraft, operators shall download and review EVMS data after every flight. Any N2 vibration reading between 2.0 mm/s and 3.0 mm/s shall be reported to the engine manufacturer and DGCA within 72 hours.
3. **Repetitive Inspection**: All affected engines shall undergo a detailed borescope inspection of the N2 bearing at intervals not exceeding **1,500 flight hours** or **12 months**, whichever comes first, until a terminating action (replacement with the improved bearing assembly per Service Bulletin GEnx-1B-72-0148 or Trent 1000-72-AG267) is accomplished.
4. **Terminating Action**: Installation of the improved N2 bearing assembly constitutes the terminating action for the repetitive inspection requirement.

### Compliance
- Initial compliance within **90 days** of the effective date of this AD or before the aircraft accumulates 1,500 flight hours since the last N2 bearing inspection, whichever comes first.
- Non-compliance renders the aircraft unairworthy. Dispatch with a known N2 vibration exceedance above 3.0 mm/s is prohibited.

### Impact on Dispatch
Pre-flight dispatch systems shall include an automated check of the EVMS N2 vibration data. If the most recent data shows N2 vibration above 3.0 mm/s, the aircraft shall be flagged as **unfit for dispatch** and routed to maintenance.

## AD-2025-A321-015: Airbus A321neo Hydraulic System Pressure Check

**Effective Date**: 15 September 2025
**Applicability**: All Airbus A321-200neo (A321neo) aircraft with PW1100G-JM engines, serial numbers 8001 through 11500.
**Supersedes**: AD-2024-A321-009

### Background
Service experience has revealed intermittent hydraulic pressure fluctuations in the Green hydraulic system on certain A321neo aircraft. The fluctuations have been traced to a manufacturing defect in the engine-driven pump (EDP) housing that may allow internal bypass, leading to reduced system pressure during high-demand phases (gear extension, flap operation).

### Required Actions
1. **Pre-Flight Check**: Before each flight, the flight crew shall verify Green hydraulic system pressure is within the normal range (2,800–3,100 PSI) during the pre-flight hydraulic test. If pressure drops below **2,750 PSI** during the ground check, the aircraft shall not be dispatched.
2. **Detailed Inspection**: Within **500 flight hours** or **6 months** of the effective date (whichever comes first), all affected aircraft shall undergo a detailed inspection of the Green system EDP per Airbus Service Bulletin A321neo-29-1047.
3. **Replacement**: If inspection reveals any defect in the EDP housing, the pump shall be replaced with the modified unit (Part Number HYD-EDP-321-MOD2) before further flight.
4. **Repetitive Inspection**: Until the modified EDP is installed, repeat the detailed inspection every **300 flight hours**.

### Compliance
- Mandatory. No extension permitted without prior written DGCA approval.
- Operators shall update their MEL to reflect that the Green system EDP is not deferrable on affected aircraft until the modified pump is installed.

## AD-2026-GEN-003: RVSM Equipment Compliance Check

**Effective Date**: 01 January 2026
**Applicability**: All aircraft approved for operations in Reduced Vertical Separation Minimum (RVSM) airspace (FL290 to FL410).
**Supersedes**: AD-2023-GEN-001

### Background
Following multiple incidents of altitude deviations in RVSM airspace within the Mumbai and Delhi FIRs, DGCA has mandated enhanced verification of RVSM equipment functionality.

### Required Actions
1. **Pre-Flight RVSM Check**: For any flight planned at or above FL290, the flight crew shall verify before dispatch:
   - Both primary altimeters agree within **75 feet** of each other.
   - The altitude alerting system is functional.
   - The automatic altitude-hold function of the autopilot is operational and captures and holds the assigned altitude within **65 feet**.
   - The transponder altitude reporting (Mode C/S) is functional and agrees with the primary altimeters within **125 feet**.
2. **Biannual Equipment Verification**: All RVSM-approved aircraft shall undergo a comprehensive RVSM equipment verification check every **6 months**, including static source error correction (SSEC) check and altimeter calibration.
3. **Height Monitoring**: Operators shall participate in the RVSM height monitoring program and ensure at least **2 height monitoring events per aircraft per year**. Aircraft that fail to achieve the required monitoring events shall have their RVSM approval suspended.

### Compliance
- Non-compliance with pre-flight RVSM checks: The flight shall be re-planned to remain below FL290 or the aircraft shall not be dispatched for the planned route.
- Biannual verification: Must be completed within 30 days of the due date. Overdue verification renders the RVSM approval invalid.

### Impact on Dispatch
The dispatch system shall verify that the aircraft has a current RVSM equipment verification certificate before assigning any flight plan with cruise altitude at or above FL290. Expired RVSM certifications must trigger automatic re-routing below FL290 or aircraft substitution.

## AD-2025-B777-008: Boeing 777-300ER Landing Gear Inspection

**Effective Date**: 01 July 2025
**Applicability**: All Boeing 777-300ER aircraft with landing gear assemblies manufactured before January 2024 (pre-modification gear per Boeing Service Bulletin 777-32-0189).
**Supersedes**: None

### Background
Structural analysis and in-service findings on several Boeing 777 aircraft have identified fatigue cracking in the main landing gear (MLG) side brace link. If undetected, this cracking could progress to failure of the side brace, resulting in gear collapse during landing.

### Required Actions
1. **Initial Inspection**: Within **1,000 flight cycles** or **12 months** of the effective date (whichever comes first), perform a detailed visual inspection and fluorescent penetrant inspection (FPI) of the MLG side brace link assembly on both main gear.
2. **Repetitive Inspection**: If no cracking is found, repeat the inspection at intervals not exceeding **750 flight cycles** or **9 months**, whichever comes first.
3. **Corrective Action**: If any crack is detected, the side brace link shall be replaced before the next flight. The aircraft shall not be dispatched with a cracked side brace link.
4. **Terminating Action**: Replacement of the side brace link with the improved assembly (Boeing P/N 777-MLG-SBL-MOD) per Service Bulletin 777-32-0189 terminates the repetitive inspection requirement.

### Compliance
- Mandatory. This AD is classified as **Priority** due to the safety-critical nature of the landing gear structure.
- Records of each inspection shall be maintained and available for DGCA audit.

## AD-2026-A320-012: Airbus A320 Family Fuel Quantity Indication System

**Effective Date**: 01 March 2026
**Applicability**: All Airbus A318, A319, A320, and A321 (CEO and NEO variants) aircraft.

### Background
Erroneous fuel quantity indications have been reported on multiple A320-family aircraft, attributed to a software anomaly in the Fuel Quantity Indication Computer (FQIC) that may display higher fuel quantity than actual under certain temperature conditions (OAT below -30C at cruise altitude).

### Required Actions
1. **Software Update**: Install FQIC software version 4.2.1 or later (per Airbus Service Bulletin A320-28-1392) within **180 days** of the effective date.
2. **Interim Procedure**: Until the software update is installed, for any flight where the en-route OAT is forecast to be below -30C, the crew shall cross-check the FQIC indication with the manual fuel dipstick measurement before departure and compare fuel burn with flight plan predictions at waypoints. Any discrepancy exceeding **500 kg** shall be treated as a fuel emergency requiring diversion.
3. **Pre-Dispatch Check**: The dispatch system shall flag any A320-family aircraft without the updated FQIC software when the route forecast includes OAT below -30C at cruise altitude.

### Compliance
- Software update: Within 180 days. No extension without DGCA approval.
- Interim procedure: Effective immediately.

## AD-2025-B737-022: Boeing 737-800/737 MAX Rudder Trim Actuator

**Effective Date**: 15 November 2025
**Applicability**: Boeing 737-800 and 737 MAX 8 aircraft with rudder trim actuators manufactured by supplier XYZ (Part Numbers RTA-737-001 through RTA-737-450).

### Background
Reports of rudder trim anomalies have been traced to actuators with potential internal corrosion due to inadequate sealing. A sticking or jammed rudder trim actuator could result in asymmetric flight conditions.

### Required Actions
1. **Identification**: Within **30 days**, identify all affected aircraft by cross-referencing the rudder trim actuator part number and serial number with the affected range per Boeing Service Bulletin 737-27-1289.
2. **Inspection**: Within **500 flight hours** or **6 months** (whichever comes first), perform a functional test of the rudder trim actuator per the maintenance manual procedure.
3. **Replacement**: If the functional test reveals any anomaly (binding, excessive friction, intermittent operation), replace the actuator with the improved sealed unit (P/N RTA-737-501).
4. **Repetitive Test**: Until replaced, repeat the functional test every **250 flight hours**.

### Compliance
- Mandatory. Aircraft with a failed functional test shall not be dispatched until the actuator is replaced.

## AD-2026-B787-005: Boeing 787 Lithium-Ion Battery Thermal Monitoring

**Effective Date**: 15 March 2026
**Applicability**: All Boeing 787-8 and 787-9 aircraft.
**Supersedes**: AD-2024-B787-003

### Background
Enhanced monitoring requirements for the main and APU lithium-ion battery systems, following updated thermal runway analysis by Boeing.

### Required Actions
1. **Pre-Flight Battery Check**: Before each dispatch, the Battery Monitoring Unit (BMU) data shall be reviewed. If any cell shows:
   - Voltage imbalance exceeding **0.15V** between cells, or
   - Temperature exceeding **60C** during the previous charge cycle, or
   - Capacity degradation exceeding **20%** from nominal,
   the aircraft shall not be dispatched until the battery is replaced or cleared by engineering.
2. **Recurring Check**: Download and analyze BMU data at intervals not exceeding **100 flight hours** or **30 days**, whichever comes first.
3. **Battery Replacement**: Replace both main and APU batteries at intervals not exceeding **18 months** or **2,500 flight hours**, whichever comes first (reduced from the previous 24-month / 3,000-hour interval).

### Compliance
- Pre-flight check: Effective immediately.
- Replacement interval: Effective within 60 days of the AD effective date.

## AD-2026-ATR-002: ATR 72-600 Propeller De-Icing System

**Effective Date**: 01 April 2026
**Applicability**: All ATR 72-600 aircraft operating in conditions where icing is forecast or encountered.

### Background
In-service reports indicate that the propeller de-icing system on certain ATR 72-600 aircraft may exhibit intermittent failures due to wiring chafing in the propeller hub area. This could lead to asymmetric propeller icing and associated handling difficulties.

### Required Actions
1. **Wiring Inspection**: Within **300 flight hours** or **3 months**, inspect the propeller de-icing wiring harness per ATR Service Bulletin ATR72-61-1025.
2. **Wiring Replacement**: If any chafing, cracking, or damage is found, replace the wiring harness before the next flight.
3. **Pre-Flight Functional Test**: Before dispatch into known or forecast icing conditions, perform a functional test of the propeller de-icing system. If the system does not pass the functional test, the aircraft shall not be dispatched into icing conditions.
4. **Repetitive Inspection**: Repeat the wiring inspection every **600 flight hours** until the improved wiring harness (P/N ATR-PDI-WH-MOD) is installed.

### Compliance
- Mandatory for all operations into icing conditions. Dispatch into icing conditions with an inoperative propeller de-icing system is prohibited under all circumstances.

## AD-2025-GEN-011: Emergency Locator Transmitter (ELT) Battery Replacement

**Effective Date**: 01 October 2025
**Applicability**: All aircraft registered in India equipped with ELTs using lithium battery packs.

### Background
Manufacturer safety bulletin indicates that certain lithium battery packs for ELTs may have reduced shelf life, potentially resulting in the ELT failing to transmit at full power during an emergency.

### Required Actions
1. **Battery Check**: Within **60 days**, verify the manufacturing date and lot number of the installed ELT battery against the affected batch list (per ELT manufacturer Service Bulletin ELT-BAT-2025-01).
2. **Replacement**: If the battery is from an affected batch, replace it within **90 days** of the effective date.
3. **Functional Test**: After battery replacement, perform a full ELT functional test per the maintenance manual.
4. **Ongoing**: ELT battery replacement intervals shall not exceed **24 months** for the affected battery type (reduced from 36 months).

### Compliance
- Mandatory. An aircraft with an ELT battery from the affected batch that has not been replaced by the compliance date shall not be dispatched.

## AD-2026-GEN-007: Cockpit Voice Recorder and Flight Data Recorder Power Supply

**Effective Date**: 15 February 2026
**Applicability**: All aircraft with more than 19 passenger seats.

### Background
Analysis of several incident investigations revealed that CVR/FDR independent power supply units were non-functional due to capacitor degradation, resulting in loss of recorded data after main power interruption.

### Required Actions
1. **Inspection**: Within **6 months** of the effective date, inspect the CVR and FDR independent power supply units for capacitor condition per the equipment manufacturer's service bulletin.
2. **Replacement**: If capacitor ESR (Equivalent Series Resistance) exceeds the manufacturer's specified limit, replace the power supply unit before the next flight.
3. **Repetitive Inspection**: Inspect power supply units every **12 months** until the improved power supply (with solid-state capacitors) is installed.
4. **Pre-Dispatch Requirement**: CVR and FDR functionality shall be verified as part of the daily first-flight pre-flight check. Aircraft with inoperative CVR or FDR shall not be dispatched (no MEL relief for CVR/FDR on aircraft with >19 seats).

### Compliance
- Mandatory. No deferral permitted.
